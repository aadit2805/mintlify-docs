GitaChat lets you bring any question — about fear, purpose, duty, or grief — to one of the world's most enduring wisdom texts and receive guidance drawn directly from it. Unlike a keyword search that hunts for matching words, GitaChat understands the *meaning* of what you ask. It converts your question into a semantic embedding and searches across all 700 verses of the Bhagavad Gita to surface the one that genuinely speaks to your situation. Alongside that verse you receive two layers of commentary: an AI-generated reflection written specifically for the question you asked, and the authentic traditional scholarly interpretation — so you always see both the living relevance and the original meaning of the teaching.

## The Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Sanskrit scripture organized into 18 chapters, set as a dialogue between the warrior Arjuna and his charioteer Krishna on the eve of a great battle. It explores some of the deepest questions a person can face: What is my duty? How do I act rightly in the face of uncertainty? What is the nature of the self? Composed as part of the Indian epic Mahabharata, the Gita has been studied, translated, and practiced across cultures for thousands of years and continues to offer practical and philosophical guidance to readers everywhere.
