Bookmarks let you build your own personal anthology of Bhagavad Gita verses — the ones that spoke to you, the ones you want to return to, and the ones you want to sit with for a while. Every verse you save is collected in one place, ready to revisit whenever you need it. You can also attach a personal note to any saved verse to capture your thoughts in the moment.

## Saving a verse

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a verse">
    Discover a verse through [semantic search](/gitachat/search) or the [Daily Verse](/gitachat/daily-verse) page. Any verse displayed in GitaChat can be bookmarked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the lotus icon">
    Below the verse translation and commentary, click the **lotus icon** in the verse actions bar. The icon is outlined when the verse is not yet saved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verse saved">
    The lotus icon fills in to indicate the verse is now in your collection. The bookmark is saved immediately — no confirmation step required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing your bookmarks

Navigate to your saved verses at any time:

- Click **Saved** in the navigation bar, or
- Go directly to [gitachat.org/saved](https://gitachat.org/saved)

Your Saved page shows all bookmarked verses in the order you saved them, each displaying the chapter and verse number along with the first lines of the translation. Click any entry to open the full verse with its commentary.

## Notes

You can attach a personal note to any verse — a reflection, a question it raised for you, or a reminder of the context in which you found it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the verse">
    Navigate to any verse through search, the Daily Verse page, or your Saved list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the note editor">
    Click the **Add Note** button (pencil icon) in the verse actions bar. A modal will open where you can write your thoughts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your note">
    Type your reflection and click **Save**. Your note is stored with the verse and can be edited or deleted at any time by clicking the **Note** button again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Notes are a good place to record what life situation led you to a verse — so when you return to it later, you remember why it mattered to you.
</Tip>

## Removing bookmarks

To remove a verse from your saved collection, click the **filled lotus icon** on the verse — either from the Saved page or from the verse itself anywhere on the site. The icon returns to its outlined state and the verse is removed from your collection immediately.

<Info>
  Bookmarks and notes require a free GitaChat account. Sign in to sync your collection across all your devices and browsers. Without signing in, bookmarks are stored locally in your browser and may be lost if you clear your browser data or switch devices.
</Info>
