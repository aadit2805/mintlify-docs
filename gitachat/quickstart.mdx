In this guide you will go from a question on your mind to a relevant Bhagavad Gita verse, an AI commentary written for exactly what you asked, and the classical scholarly interpretation — all in under a minute. You will also learn how to bookmark verses, add personal notes, and generate images so you can hold on to teachings that matter to you.

## Search for Guidance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit GitaChat">
    Open [gitachat.org](https://gitachat.org) in your browser. The search box appears in the center of the page with the prompt *"What is troubling you?"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type your question">
    Enter whatever is on your mind — a personal struggle, a philosophical question, or a feeling you cannot quite name. For example:

    - *"How do I find inner peace?"*
    - *"What is my purpose in life?"*
    - *"How to overcome fear?"*

    If you are not sure where to start, click one of the **suggested prompt** buttons below the search box to fill the field automatically, then press **Ask**.

    <Tip>
      Ask a personal, specific question rather than a broad one. Because the AI commentary is written in direct response to what you type, the more honestly you phrase your situation, the more precisely the guidance will address it.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the best-match verse">
    GitaChat returns the single most semantically relevant verse from the Gita's 700 verses. At the top of the result you see the **chapter and verse number** (for example, *Chapter 2, Verse 47*), followed by the full English translation of that verse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the AI commentary">
    Below the verse, under the heading **For You**, you will find a commentary generated specifically for the question you asked. This is not a pre-written summary — it is a fresh reflection that connects the verse's teaching to the concern you brought to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand the traditional commentary">
    Next to the AI reflection, under **Traditional Commentary**, you will find the classical scholarly interpretation of the verse. Read this alongside the AI commentary to see both the timeless scholarly meaning and its personal relevance to your question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore related verses">
    Below the main result, GitaChat surfaces additional verses that are semantically close to your question. Select any of them to read its translation and explore a broader range of the Gita's teaching on your topic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the verse or take action">
    Use the action buttons below the commentary to engage further with the verse:

    - **Bookmark** (lotus icon) — saves the verse to your account so you can return to it any time.
    - **Visualize** — generates a piece of visual art inspired by the verse, which you can download or share.
    - **Add Note** — lets you write a private personal reflection attached to the verse.
    - **Share** — copies a direct link to the verse to your clipboard.

    <Note>
      Bookmarking, notes, and image generation require you to be signed in. See [Sign in for more](#sign-in-for-more) below to learn how to create an account.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Suggested Prompts

The following prompts are built into GitaChat and appear as quick-start buttons on the home page:

```text
How do I find inner peace?
What is my purpose in life?
How to overcome fear?
What is true happiness?
```

You are not limited to these. GitaChat handles open-ended personal questions, philosophical inquiries, and everything in between — the semantic search works best when you write naturally, exactly as you would ask a trusted teacher.

## Sign in for More

GitaChat works without an account, but signing in with your email unlocks a fuller experience:

- **Permanent bookmarks** — your saved verses are stored in the cloud and available on any device.
- **Search history** — every question you ask is logged so you can revisit past searches and the verses they returned.
- **Personal notes** — attach private reflections to any verse and find them again whenever you return.
- **Daily verse emails** — subscribe to receive a verse and commentary in your inbox each morning.

Select **Sign in** in the top-right corner of the page to create an account or log in with an existing one.
