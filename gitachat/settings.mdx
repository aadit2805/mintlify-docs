The Settings page is your control panel for your GitaChat email subscription. Reach it at any time by navigating to [gitachat.org/settings](https://gitachat.org/settings).

## Account

GitaChat uses **Clerk** for authentication, which means you can sign in securely without GitaChat ever storing your password.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Signing in">
    Click **Sign in** from the navigation bar or any page that requires an account. Clerk supports sign-in via email, Google, and other providers. Once signed in, your bookmarks, notes, and search history are tied to your account and sync across devices.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Signing out">
    Click your profile icon in the navigation bar and select **Sign out**. Your bookmarks and history remain saved in your account and will be waiting when you sign back in.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Email subscription

Manage your daily verse email from the Settings page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Go to [gitachat.org/settings](https://gitachat.org/settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Daily Verse Email section">
    The **Daily Verse Email** toggle is the primary control. When the toggle is on (saffron), you are subscribed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your timezone">
    When subscribed, a timezone selector appears below the toggle. GitaChat sends emails at **8:00 AM in your selected timezone**. Choose from a list of common timezones, or leave it as your auto-detected local timezone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unsubscribe">
    Toggle the switch off to stop receiving daily verse emails immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you travel across timezones frequently, update your timezone in Settings to keep receiving emails at 8:00 AM local time wherever you are.
</Tip>

## Search history

GitaChat saves your past searches so you can revisit any verse you found, along with the exact question that led you there.

- Navigate to [gitachat.org/history](https://gitachat.org/history) to view your full search history.
- Each entry shows the date and time, your original query, and the chapter and verse returned.
- Click any history entry to re-open the full verse with its commentary, exactly as it appeared when you first found it.
- To remove all past searches, click **Clear** at the top of the history page and confirm. This action cannot be undone.

<Info>
  Search history requires a signed-in account. Without signing in, your most recent search is saved temporarily in your browser's local storage but is not persisted long-term.
</Info>

## Data and privacy

GitaChat stores your search history and bookmarks securely. Your data is associated with your account and is never sold or shared with third parties.

- Your email address (if subscribed) is used only to send the daily verse email.
- You can delete your search history at any time from the History page.
- To request full deletion of your account data, contact GitaChat support.
