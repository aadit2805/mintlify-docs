Every verse in GitaChat comes with two distinct commentaries shown side by side. The first is written specifically for you — a response to your question that draws on the verse's teaching and applies it to the situation you described. The second is the traditional interpretation of the verse, grounded in the Gita's own philosophical tradition. Together, they give you both the timeless meaning and the personal relevance.

## AI commentary — "For You"

The **For You** commentary is generated fresh for each search by GPT-4o-mini. It reads your question, finds the matching verse, and then writes an explanation that bridges the Gita's teaching to the specific situation you brought to it.

This means the same verse can carry different commentary depending on how you asked:

- Searching **"How do I handle failure?"** and **"How do I stay motivated?"** might both return the same verse about performing action without attachment to results — but the commentary will speak to failure in the first case and to motivation in the second.

<Tip>
  Read the **For You** commentary to see how the verse applies to your specific situation, then read the Traditional Commentary below it for the canonical teaching that has guided practitioners for centuries.
</Tip>

The **For You** commentary updates every time you search, even for the same verse — because your question changes what matters most about what the verse is saying.

## Traditional commentary

The **Traditional Commentary** is the authentic, pre-computed scholarly interpretation of the verse — consistent across all searches and grounded in the historical understanding of the Gita.

- It does not change based on your question.
- It reflects the verse's meaning as it has been taught and understood in the Gita's own tradition.
- It gives you a stable, canonical reference point alongside the personalized AI reflection.

On longer verses, the traditional commentary may be lengthy. It is displayed in full — scroll within its column to read it completely.

## Verse images

GitaChat can generate an original AI artwork inspired by any verse.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a verse">
    Retrieve a verse through search or the daily verse page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Visualize">
    In the verse actions bar below the commentary, click **Visualize** (indicated by a sparkle icon). GitaChat will show a brief "Creating…" indicator while the image is generated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View and share your image">
    The generated artwork opens in a modal. Each image is unique — generated via Replicate based on the verse's translation. You can share the image or close the modal and click **View Image** to reopen it at any time during your session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Image generation requires you to be signed in. Each image is unique and is not cached permanently — generating the same verse twice may produce different artwork.
</Info>
