Your first simulation result is a starting point, not a verdict. The What-If panel lets you push individual variables — spending and timeline — and instantly see how each change shifts your probability of success. Instead of wondering whether a budget cut or more time matters more, you get the exact answer expressed as a probability change, ranked by impact.

<Info>
  What-if scenarios run the same full simulation engine as your original simulation. Every probability figure in the panel reflects a complete Monte Carlo run against your adjusted parameters — not an estimate or a linear approximation.
</Info>

## Available adjustments

Drift tests the following scenario types automatically after every simulation, and surfaces them ranked by the size of their probability impact.

| Adjustment | What it models |
|---|---|
| **Spending −10%** | Monthly spending reduced by 10% — the result of trimming discretionary categories |
| **Spending −20%** | Monthly spending reduced by 20% — a more aggressive budget tightening |
| **Spending +10%** | Monthly spending increased by 10% — shows the cost of lifestyle creep on your plan |
| **Timeline +6 months** | Goal deadline extended by 6 months — more time compounds savings and investment returns |
| **Timeline +12 months** | Goal deadline extended by 12 months — the largest timeline extension Drift tests by default |

When you connect Plaid-linked accounts, Drift also generates **category-level scenarios** based on your actual spending data. For example: *"Cut Dining by 35% (~$280/mo)"* with a projected probability impact calculated against your real spending mix.

## How to use what-if

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run your initial simulation">
    Complete the goal-setting flow and wait for the simulation to finish. Your baseline success probability and percentile outcomes appear on the results page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the What-If Analysis panel">
    Scroll down on the results page to the **What-If Analysis** section. Drift populates it automatically — no extra action needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the ranked scenarios">
    The table lists every scenario alongside the baseline probability, the adjusted probability, and the percentage-point impact. Scenarios are sorted from highest to lowest impact so the most effective lever is always at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act on the highest-impact move">
    Check the **Highest-impact moves** cards beneath the table. These highlight the top two scenarios from your analysis and are the fastest way to identify a single actionable change — whether that's cutting a specific spending category or extending your timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the results

The What-If table always shows your **current plan** as the baseline row, so every other scenario is directly comparable.

| Column | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Scenario** | A plain-English description of the change being modeled |
| **Change** | The specific parameter adjustment (e.g., `-10% total spending`) |
| **Probability** | Your new success probability if you make this change |
| **Impact** | The percentage-point difference from your baseline (green = improvement, red = decline) |

A positive impact badge — for example, `+14%` — means making that change raises your success probability by 14 percentage points. If your baseline is 58%, that adjustment takes you to 72%.

<Tip>
  If you have Plaid-linked accounts, compare the category-level scenarios against the aggregate spending scenarios. Often, a targeted cut to one high-spend category produces a larger improvement than an across-the-board reduction — and is easier to act on.
</Tip>
