Drift doesn't ask you to fill out a form with numbers you may not know off the top of your head. Instead, you describe what you're trying to accomplish — in plain English, typed or spoken — and Drift's AI pulls out the goal parameters automatically. Tell Drift you want to save for a house down payment, and it figures out the amount, the timeline, and how to classify the goal. You never touch a spreadsheet.

## Text input

Type your goal in natural language in the goal input field. Drift parses it and kicks off the simulation immediately once the key parameters are confirmed.

```text Goal examples
Save $50,000 for a house down payment in 3 years
Pay off $15,000 in credit card debt in 18 months
Build a $100,000 emergency fund in 5 years
Retire comfortably in 30 years
Save $8,000 for a vacation by next December
Pay off my student loans in 4 years
```

If your input is ambiguous — for example, "buy a car" without a price — Drift asks a clarifying question rather than guessing. For example: *"A car can range widely in price. Did you have a target amount in mind, like $25,000 or $40,000?"* You can answer inline and continue without re-typing your original goal.

<Tip>
  The more specific your goal, the more accurate the simulation. Always include a dollar amount and a timeframe — "save $30,000 in 2 years" produces a sharper result than "save for a car."
</Tip>

## Voice input

Click the microphone on the goal page and speak your goal out loud. Drift handles the full speech-to-simulation pipeline automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Speak your goal">
    Click the mic button and describe your goal in a natural sentence. Drift transcribes your speech to text in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI parses the parameters">
    Drift reads the transcript and extracts your target amount, timeline, and goal type. If it needs a clarification, it asks a single follow-up question — spoken back to you in audio.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and simulate">
    Once Drift has all three parameters, it summarizes what it understood — *"Got it: $50,000 for a house down payment in 3 years. Let's see what your odds look like!"* — and automatically launches the simulation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The voice conversation is multi-turn: Drift can ask and answer up to several follow-up questions before all parameters are locked in. You never have to restart because you forgot to mention the amount or the timeline.

## What Drift extracts from your goal

Regardless of whether you type or speak, Drift extracts the same three structured parameters before running the simulation.

| Field | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Target amount** | The dollar figure you need to reach | `$50,000` |
| **Timeline** | How many months you have to reach it | `36 months` (3 years) |
| **Goal type** | How your goal is categorized | House down payment, debt payoff, emergency fund, retirement, travel, education, investment, or a custom goal |

These parameters drive every aspect of the simulation: success is defined as reaching the target amount within the timeline, and the goal type helps Drift frame its recommendations in context.

<Note>
  If Drift can't confidently determine the target amount or timeline from your input, it asks a clarifying question before proceeding. Drift will never silently substitute a placeholder amount for your real goal.
</Note>
