Drift's AI advisor is a conversational financial coach that's present at every step — from the moment you describe your goal to the moment you're reading your results. It has full context about your accounts, income, spending patterns, and simulation output. Ask it anything about your plan and it answers in plain language, not jargon.

## Goal conversation

When you set a goal by voice, Drift's advisor doesn't just listen once and guess — it guides a multi-turn conversation to make sure every required parameter is captured before the simulation starts.

- The advisor opens with a short, friendly prompt and waits for you to describe your goal in your own words.
- If the goal is missing the target amount, timeline, or goal type, it asks one focused question at a time: *"Nice — how much are you thinking for the down payment?"* then *"And when are you hoping to buy?"*
- Once all three parameters are confirmed, the advisor summarizes what it understood: *"Perfect — $50,000 for a house down payment in 3 years. Let's see what your odds look like!"* The simulation starts immediately.
- Every response is kept to one or two sentences so the conversation moves quickly whether you're typing or listening.

<Note>
  The goal conversation maintains a full history of the exchange. If you change your mind mid-conversation — "actually, make it $60,000" — the advisor updates the extracted parameters without losing the rest of the context.
</Note>

## Results advisor

After your simulation runs, the **Chat with Advisor** button appears in the bottom-right corner of the results page. Click it to open a chat panel where you can ask follow-up questions about your specific numbers.

The advisor has complete context for your session, including:

- Your success probability and all five percentile outcomes (p10–p90)
- Your median outcome and gap from the goal
- Monthly income, spending, savings rate, and liquid assets
- Credit debt, loan debt, and monthly loan payments
- Spending by category (when Plaid-linked), with monthly and annual amounts per category
- The goal type, target amount, and timeline

Example questions to ask:

```text Questions for the results advisor
What's the biggest risk to my plan?
How much more should I save each month to hit 80% probability?
What happens if I get a 10% raise next year?
Which spending category is hurting me the most?
How much would I need to cut on dining to close my gap?
Is my emergency fund situation okay?
```

Responses reference your actual numbers — not generic advice. If you ask about dining, the advisor tells you exactly how much you're spending on dining per month and calculates a specific cut amount.

## Voice interaction

The advisor supports full voice interaction, not just text.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the chat panel">
    Click **Chat with Advisor** on the results page. The panel opens with an initial message from the advisor confirming it has reviewed your results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the microphone to speak">
    Press the mic button and ask your question out loud. Drift transcribes your speech and processes your question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hear the response">
    The advisor's reply appears as text in the chat and is simultaneously read aloud. A pulsing speaker icon indicates when audio is playing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue the conversation">
    Ask follow-up questions — voice or text — and the advisor maintains the full conversation history. Each response takes your previous questions and its own prior answers into account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Text and voice can be mixed freely within the same conversation. Switch between typing and speaking at any point without losing context.

<Tip>
  Ask the advisor to explain the p10/p90 spread in your results. It tells you the dollar difference between a bad year and a great year — and what specific factors in your profile drive that spread. This is often the clearest way to understand the real risk in your plan.
</Tip>
