The Capital One Nessie API is a developer sandbox that provides realistic but entirely fictional bank accounts, transaction histories, and loan data. If you want to explore Drift's simulation features before linking your real accounts — or if you're evaluating Drift without a supported bank — Nessie lets you connect instantly and start running simulations against a pre-built financial profile right away.

## What You Get with Nessie

When you connect through Nessie, Drift loads a complete sandbox financial profile that includes all the data types the simulation engine needs.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Checking and savings accounts" icon="building-columns">
    Drift reads your sandbox checking and savings balances as liquid assets and uses them as the starting cash position in your simulation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Purchases and deposits" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left">
    Your sandbox transaction history includes merchant purchases, payroll-style deposits, and other activity that Drift uses to estimate monthly income and spending.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bills" icon="file-invoice-dollar">
    Recurring bill payments in the Nessie sandbox feed into Drift's fixed expense calculation, giving the simulation a realistic baseline for your monthly obligations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Loans" icon="landmark">
    Sandbox loan accounts — home, auto, and small business — contribute outstanding balances and monthly payment amounts to the simulation's debt modeling.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How to Connect

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the login screen and select Demo Mode">
    On the Drift sign-in page, click the **Demo Mode** tab. You can either choose one of the three pre-built demo profiles displayed on screen, or enter your own Nessie customer ID manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a demo profile or enter your own customer ID">
    To get started instantly, click any of the pre-built demo profiles — each shows estimated salary, expenses, debt, and net worth so you can pick one that matches the scenario you want to explore. Drift validates the selection and connects automatically.

    Alternatively, paste your own 24-character Capital One Nessie customer ID into the **Customer ID** field and click **Connect Account**. Drift validates the ID against the Nessie API before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drift loads your sandbox accounts">
    Drift fetches all accounts and transaction history associated with the customer ID — checking, savings, purchases, deposits, bills, and loans — and builds your financial overview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a goal and run a simulation">
    Head to the dashboard to review your sandbox financial profile, then navigate to the goal screen to set a target amount, timeline, and risk tolerance. Drift runs your Monte Carlo simulation against the Nessie data exactly as it would with a real bank connection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Nessie vs. Plaid

Use this table to decide which connection method fits your needs.

| Feature | Nessie Sandbox | Plaid (Real Bank) |
|---|---|---|
| **Account types** | Checking, savings, loans, purchases | Checking, savings, credit cards, loans, investments |
| **Transaction data** | Simulated | Real (90 days) |
| **Income detection** | Derived from sandbox deposits | Auto-detected from actual deposit patterns |
| **Spending volatility** | Calculated from simulated purchases | Calculated from real transaction variance |
| **Credit card modeling** | Not available | Per-card APR and minimum payment |
| **Investment allocation** | Not available | Real holdings and asset mix |
| **Best for** | Exploring Drift risk-free | Full simulation accuracy |

<Tip>
  Use Nessie to get familiar with Drift's goal-setting flow, simulation output, and results page before you connect your real accounts. Everything works the same way — you just swap in your Plaid connection when you're ready for accuracy.
</Tip>

<Info>
  To use your own Nessie customer ID, sign up at the [Capital One Nessie developer portal](http://www.nessieisreal.com). Once you create a customer and seed it with accounts, use the returned customer ID to connect in Drift's Demo Mode. If you just want to explore Drift quickly, skip this step and use one of the pre-built demo profiles instead.
</Info>
