Drift gives you control over the financial inputs and simulation defaults that shape every forecast you run. From your risk tolerance to which bank accounts are connected, the settings you configure here directly influence how Drift models your financial future. This page walks you through each configurable area so you can tailor the experience to your situation.

## Financial Profile Settings

Your financial profile tells Drift how to model your income, spending habits, and investment behavior. These settings are applied every time you start a new simulation.

### Risk Tolerance

Risk tolerance controls the investment return assumptions Drift uses in your Monte Carlo simulation. You set it on **Step 1** of the goal input screen — alongside your age — each time you start a new simulation. The level you choose is remembered as your default for the next session.

| Level | What it means for your simulation |
|---|---|
| **Low** | Uses conservative return assumptions (4% annual mean). Produces tighter, more predictable outcome ranges. Best for short-term goals or cautious savers. |
| **Medium** | Uses balanced return assumptions (7% annual mean). Moderate spread between optimistic and pessimistic outcomes. Suited to most investors. |
| **High** | Uses aggressive return assumptions (10% annual mean). Produces a wider range of outcomes — higher upside potential, but also greater downside risk. |

See [Risk Profiles](/configuration/risk-profiles) for a full breakdown of how each level affects your probability and outcome distribution.

## Connected Accounts

Drift uses bank account data to derive your monthly income, spending patterns, and debt picture. The more accurate this data, the more precise your simulation results.

### Plaid-Connected Accounts

If you connected a real bank account during onboarding, Drift pulls balances and up to 90 days of transaction history using Plaid's read-only access. You can view all linked accounts from the **Accounts** section of your dashboard.

From there you can:

- **View account details** — see which accounts are active and when they were last synced
- **Re-sync account data** — trigger a fresh pull of your latest balances and transactions
- **Unlink an account** — remove a connection without deleting your simulation history

### Nessie Sandbox Accounts

If you signed up without linking a real bank account, Drift connects to the Capital One Nessie sandbox — a simulated banking environment with realistic account structures and transaction data. This lets you explore all of Drift's features without exposing real financial information.

You can switch between sandbox and Plaid real accounts at any time from the **Accounts** section. Switching data sources will prompt you to re-run any active simulations.

### Re-Syncing Account Data

Drift analyzes the most recent 90 days of transactions to derive your spending patterns, income stability score, and volatility estimate. To refresh this analysis with your latest data:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your dashboard">
    Open the main dashboard from the Drift navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Accounts">
    Select **Accounts** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trigger a re-sync">
    Click **Sync now** next to the account you want to refresh. Drift will fetch your latest transactions and update your financial profile automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-run your simulation">
    After syncing, navigate to any saved goal and click **Re-run simulation** to apply the updated data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your financial data — including account balances, transaction history, and income patterns — is used exclusively to power your simulations within Drift. It is never sold or shared with third parties.
</Note>
